Outreach

In 2012, I co-founded McMaster's Sidewalk Astronomy where a group of graduate students set up a telescope on campus giving the public a chance to look at some of the brightest objects in Hamilton's night sky. I organize weekly nighttime public observing events on campus and monthly solar observing events. In 2013, one of our solar observing events was featured in the McMaster Daily News. I have also secured funding from the School of Graduate Studies through the SPICES scholarship. I used the funding to buy new equipment including a new 10" Dobsonian telescope for the Department of Physics and Astronomy.

In 2010, I got involved with the William J. McCallion Planetarium as a regular presenter. I give interactive private shows to all sorts of groups such as elementary and high school classes, cub scouts and even wedding engagement proposals. I also give a public show currently twice every 3-4 months which includes:

    2014: Backyard Astronomy

    2013: The Southern Night Sky Shall Rise Again

    2012: From Young to Old, Stars Age Too!

    2011-Present: Introductory Astronomy for Kids

    2011: The Hot Universe: The Shocking Truth
